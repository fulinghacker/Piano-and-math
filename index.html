MUSIC AND MATH CIRCLE



Introduction

This website introduces circle geometry by using the circle of fifths and three songs. The three songs are "Canon in C", "Canon in D" and “Never gonna give you up” by Rick Astley.

I divided the knowledge into three courses to introduce, and mixed some music knowledge into them. Finally, some extended knowledge is also provided in "Exolore more", you can challenge it if you want.


Okay, that's the introduction for the website, so sit back, relax and we will begin.



Introduction for songs

"Canon in D" was written by Johann Pachelbel. The specific creation time and background of this song are unknown. It became popular after it was discovered in the 20th century and was widely used in many pop songs such as "Rain and Tears", "Sunday Morning", "Oh Lord, Why Lord". Canon in C is a derivative of this song, with only the change of chord progression.



"Never gonna give you up" is a pop song by Rick Astley, released on July 27, 1987. The song was pretty famous after its release, but it resurged in popularity in 2007 when the internet meme "Rickroll" went viral. In this context, in 2008, Isley won the MTV Europe Music Award for Best Performance for this song.



Courses




Basic course

The basic course uses examples and pictures to explain some basic knowledge about the circle of fifths and mathematical circles.

Through this course you will learn about music of fifth's importance, some basic definations, chord and arch.

Intermediate course

The intermediate course uses examples and pictures to explain a little bit high level knowledge about mathematical circle through circle of fifth.

Through this course you will learn about center angle and inscribe angle.

Advanced course

The advanced course uses clear explanation, examples and dynamic effects to introduce some more advanced concepts in mathematical circles with music theory.

Through this course you will learn about perpendicular bisector, tangent, and how to find the center of a circle..
