Central angle

The main chord progression of the song “Canon in C” is C G Am Em F C F G. If we mark these chords in the circle of fifths, we will be surprised to find that the range of movement of the chords in this song is exactly one-fourth of the entire circle, and ∠ FOG is 90 degrees, which is vertical.

In mathematics, this angle is the central angle, which is defined as an angle formed by two radii in a circle.



If two central angles are in the same circle and the arcs corresponding to them are same, then the degree of the two central angles will be same.

For example, refer to the left picture, ∠ EOA and ∠ AOD are central angle, and arc EA = arc AD. Because both of them are central angle and arc EA = arc AD

Therefore, ∠ EOA = ∠ AOD.







Inscribe angle

Let's continue using the two endpoints of the "Canon in C" chord progression range on the circle of fifths. Then just pick a random point, conect these these points, we can get a angle.

This angle is called an inscribe angle in mathematics, and it is defined as the angle in a circle formed by two chords with their endpoints at a point.



If the endpoints of a central angles and an inscribe angle are at the same endpoints of an arc, then the degree of inscribe angle is half of central angle.

In mathematics, we call this situation subtend, to explain the example on the left, we can say:

Because central angle of FOG is 90O, the inscribe angles and central angle are subtented by arc FG, the inscribe angles are 45O.
